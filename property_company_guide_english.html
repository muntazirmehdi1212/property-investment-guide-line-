Company Formation

How to legally structure and register your property business in Pakistan
🏢
Property Dealer
Most Common Model
Act as the intermediary between buyers and sellers. Commission-based income — the most popular starting model in Pakistan. No large capital required. Starting cost: PKR 5–10 lakh.
💼
Property Investor
Capital Required
Purchase properties with your own capital, then hold or flip for profit. Excellent for long-term wealth building. Requires PKR 50 lakh+ to start meaningfully.
🏗️
Property Developer
High Risk / High Reward
Acquire land, construct, and sell. Highest profit margins but also highest risk. Requires NOC approvals, layout plans, and a minimum PKR 1 crore+ in starting capital.
🤝
Brokerage Firm
Team-Based
A professional brokerage house with multiple agents, a brand identity, and structured operations. Office + team setup cost: PKR 20–30 lakh. Scale through staff commission splits.
SECP Registration (Private Ltd) or Sole Proprietorship
Sole Proprietorship: Obtain a trade license from the local council with your NTN. Private Limited: Register via the SECP portal — minimum 2 directors, PKR 1 lakh share capital. SECP fee: PKR 1,000–5,000.
💡 Tip: Sole Proprietorship is the best starting point. Convert to Pvt Ltd once you have 5+ employees and substantial revenue.
National Tax Number (NTN) — FBR
Register your NTN on the FBR website at no cost. You will need your CNIC, a business bank account, and your business address. Annual tax return filing is mandatory for all registered businesses.
RERA License — Real Estate Regulatory Authority
Apply for a dealer or agent license from Punjab RERA or the relevant provincial authority. License fee: PKR 10,000–50,000. Renewal is required annually. Operating without a RERA license is illegal.
Trade License — Local Municipal Council
Apply at the Union Council or City District Government for your area. Annual fee: PKR 2,000–8,000. Required for all commercial businesses operating within a municipality.
Business Bank Account
Open a current business account at HBL, Meezan, UBL, or MCB. Required documents: company registration, NTN, RERA license, directors' CNICs. All commission payments should flow through this account for tax compliance.

Investment Strategy

Short-term flipping, long-term rentals, and high-ROI area selection
"Real estate is not about buying property — it is about buying opportunity at the right time, the right location, and the right price." — Pakistan Property Investment Principle

Short-Term Flipping Strategy

Identify an Under-Valued Property
Look for properties priced 10–15% below market value. Target motivated sellers — those dealing with divorce settlements, emigration, or urgent debt needs.
Complete Quick Due Diligence
Confirm clear title, no legal disputes, and proper road access. Complete this process within 3–5 days before making an offer.
Purchase and Hold 6–12 Months
Wait for market appreciation or add minor value improvements such as a boundary wall, gate, or road pavement to increase perceived value.
Sell at 20–35% Profit
Deduct all transaction costs including dealer commission and capital gains tax to calculate your true net profit.

Long-Term Rental Income Strategy

Acquire a Rental Property
Target a 3–5 marla house or a commercial plaza unit. Ideal locations include Saddar commercial zones, main market strips, and DHA commercial areas.
Tenant Selection Process
Conduct a background check, collect advance rent, and execute a notarized lease agreement. Protect the property from disputes by following proper legal protocols.
Annual Yield Calculation
Residential rental yield in Lahore: 3–5% per year. Commercial yield: 6–9% per year. Add an annual capital appreciation of 10–15% for total return calculation.

Legal Process

Sale & Purchase Procedure

The complete 10-step legal process from verification to final transfer
Step 1 — Property Ownership Verification
Verify the seller's CNIC against original documents including the Fard (title deed) and previous sale deed. Confirm the society's NOC. Check ownership history through NADRA or the PLRA online portal.
⚠️ Never accept photocopies alone. Always demand original documents plus a certified copy before proceeding.
Step 2 — Fard Milkiat (Title Document) Verification
Obtain a Fard Milkiat from the PLRA (Punjab Land Records Authority) online portal or via FBR. Check for any co-owners, outstanding heirs, or active disputes. Confirm the current registered owner's name matches the seller.
Step 3 — Market Valuation
Apply the Comparable Sales Method: compare 5–8 recent transactions of similar properties in the same area over the last 3 months. Record both the FBR value and the prevailing market value — stamp duty is calculated on the FBR value.
Step 4 — Token Money (Advance / Bayana)
Collect 5–10% of the agreed price as token money. Issue a stamped receipt. Clearly define: if the buyer backs out, the token is forfeited; if the seller backs out, they return double the amount. Include a clear timeline in the token agreement.
💡 For a PKR 1 Crore deal, the minimum token should be PKR 5–10 lakh. A low token signals a lack of buyer seriousness.
Step 5 — Agreement to Sell (Sale Agreement)
Have a lawyer draft a comprehensive sale agreement covering: full property description, agreed price, payment schedule, possession date, penalty clauses, and a dispute resolution mechanism. Execute on PKR 500–1,000 stamp paper.
Step 6 — Transfer Process
After full payment, file the transfer at the Sub-Registrar's office or the relevant society office. Both buyer and seller must be physically present with original CNICs and two witnesses.
Step 7 — Registry and Intiqal (Revenue Record Transfer)
Registry: The sale deed is formally registered at the Sub-Registrar's office. Intiqal: Ownership is transferred in the official revenue record via the Patwari or PLRA online system. Both steps are mandatory — ensure both are completed.
Step 8 — Stamp Duty and Applicable Taxes
Stamp Duty: 3% (paid by buyer). Capital Gains Tax: 1–5% (paid by seller, varies by holding period). Withholding Tax: 1% buyer + 1% seller (non-filers pay 2% each). Society transfer fee: 0.5–2% of property value.
Step 9 — Possession and Handover
Obtain a signed Possession Letter. Ensure the seller clears all outstanding utility bills (electricity, gas, water). Hand over keys and all original documents to the buyer. Transfer the WAPDA meter into the buyer's name.
Step 10 — Commission Collection
Standard commission in Pakistan: 1% from buyer and 1% from seller (some areas charge 2% each). Issue a formal receipt stating the property address, transaction amount, and both parties' CNICs. Retain a copy for your records.
🚨
Fraud Prevention — Critical Safeguards (1) Always verify the Fard Milkiat through the official PLRA online portal — never rely on a document shown by the seller alone. (2) Accept token money and all payments via bank transfer or crossed cheque — avoid cash transactions. (3) Be alert to duplicate or forged documents — Patwari record manipulation is common in certain areas. (4) For Power of Attorney deals, verify the original PoA document with NADRA before proceeding. (5) For file or Form-B deals, confirm the society's NOC and the current transfer status before any payment.

Daily Operations & Workflow

Lead generation, client management, negotiation, and deal closure

Lead Generation

Online Property Portals
Post fresh listings daily on Zameen.com, OLX.com.pk, and Graana.com. New listings generate significantly more calls. Aim to respond to all inquiries within 2 hours to maintain lead quality.
WhatsApp Property Groups
Join area-specific WhatsApp property groups and share daily listings. Build a referral network by staying active in 10–15 relevant groups. Personal relationships in these groups convert at a much higher rate.
Facebook Lead Ads
Run location-based ads targeting DHA, Bahria, and specific housing societies. Budget: PKR 500–1,000 per day. Facebook Lead Form Ads are the most effective format for property inquiries. Expected cost per lead: PKR 200–800.
Professional Referral Network
Build relationships with bank home finance departments, lawyers, chartered accountants, and architects. Offer a referral fee of 0.25–0.5% per closed deal. These referrals carry very high conversion rates.

Deal Closing Strategy

Site Visit Preparation
Confirm the client's budget and specific requirements before the visit. Show a maximum of 3 options to avoid decision fatigue. Have property keys and all related files ready before the viewing appointment.
Negotiation Technique
Use the anchoring method: present a slightly higher price to the buyer while helping the seller accept a slightly lower one. Find the mutual middle ground. Use the close: "If I can get you this price, would you be ready to proceed today?"
Creating Honest Urgency
If genuinely true, communicate real urgency — such as other interested parties. Never fabricate urgency as it damages long-term reputation. Authentic urgency, when communicated clearly, accelerates decisions.
Collect Token on the Same Day
Once a deal is verbally agreed, collect the token money on the same day. Saying "let us confirm tomorrow" is the fastest way to lose a deal. Token money equals commitment from both parties.

Protection

Risk Management

Identifying, assessing, and mitigating the key risks in property business
🔴 High Risk
Fraudulent Documents
Forged Fard, altered signatures, and seller impersonation are common in Pakistan. Prevention: Always verify ownership via the PLRA online portal, and obtain a written legal opinion before paying any token money.
🔴 High Risk
Market Crash / Downturn
Political instability, interest rate hikes, or IMF-related economic conditions can suppress property values. Prevention: Never over-leverage. Always maintain a 20% cash reserve. Diversify your portfolio across multiple cities.
🟡 Medium Risk
Litigation and Legal Disputes
Inheritance claims and court stay orders can freeze properties. Prevention: Always conduct a title search and obtain a lawyer's no-objection certificate before purchase. Pay a premium for legally clear properties — it is worth it.
🟡 Medium Risk
Cash Flow Crisis
Delayed commission payments combined with fixed overheads can create negative cash flow. Prevention: Maintain a 3-month operating expense reserve at all times. Avoid locking all capital in illiquid property assets.
🟡 Medium Risk
Staff Misconduct
Agents bypassing the company to close deals independently is a common problem. Prevention: Include non-compete clauses in employment contracts, restrict CRM access appropriately, and structure commission splits to make company loyalty the most profitable option for agents.
🟢 Low Risk
Market Competition
High dealer density in popular areas reduces individual deal flow. Prevention: Specialize in a specific niche — a single housing society, a particular property type, or a target client segment. Brand differentiation through genuine expertise is more valuable than competing on price.
🛡️
Investor Protection Framework Sign a formal MOU with every investor before accepting any funds. Accept investment amounts exclusively via bank transfer and provide a written receipt. Send monthly written progress updates to all investors. Structure the legal relationship through a Limited Partnership Agreement prepared by a qualified lawyer. All profit and loss sharing terms must be explicitly defined and agreed in writing before any capital is deployed.
